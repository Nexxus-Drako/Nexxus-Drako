# Itavran Alert System

This document defines:

- A **single‑line status code** you can use in IM status, chat, or a quick text.  
- A **plan sheet** that explains how to read the code and what actions to take at each level.

---

## 1. The One‑Line Status Code

**Format:**  
`BxMxSx/SRx/DRx-YYYY-MM-DD/HHMM`

- `Bx` = Body level (1–4)  
- `Mx` = Mind level (1–4)  
- `Sx` = Social level (1–4)  
- `SRx` = Suicide risk (1–4)  
- `DRx` = Disaster readiness (1–3)  
- `YYYY-MM-DD/HHMM` = Date and time of status (ISO 8601 date format with 24-hour time)

### Examples

- `B1M1S1/SR1/DR1-2026-05-28/1515`  
- `B2M3S1/SR2/DR1-2026-05-28/0945`  
- `B4M4S4/SR4/DR2-2026-05-28/2230`

You can shorten it publicly:

- `B1M1S1` (only mental state)  
- `SR2` (only suicide risk)  
- `DR2` (only disaster level)

---

## 2. How to Use This System

### For yourself

- Check in once or twice a day, or whenever:
   - You feel a shift in your capacity.  
   - You are about to join a chat, stream, or hangout.  
   - You are making a decision about going out, committing to tasks, or reaching out for help.

- Pick the level that best matches your current state in each domain.  
- If unsure, choose the higher number.  
- Write or update your status line wherever you can see it (e.g., chat, status, note).

### For others

- `SR3` or `SR4` = treat as serious risk and check in with care.  
- `B4M4S4`‑style codes = high distress, offer low‑pressure support.  
- `DR2` or `DR3` = local emergency prep or threat; assume reduced capacity.

---

## 3. How to Decipher the Code

### 3.1. Mental State Levels (B, M, S)

- **1 = Green / Stable**  
   Basic functioning, usual coping tools work.

- **2 = Yellow / Watchful**  
   Strain present but still manageable.

- **3 = Orange / High strain**  
   Capacity is breaking down; need support.

- **4 = Red / Crisis‑unsafe**  
   Crisis or near‑crisis state; activate crisis plan.

Example:

- `B4` = crisis or near‑crisis in body.  
- `M4` = crisis or near‑crisis in mind.  
- `S4` = no social capacity at all.

---

### 3.2. Suicide Risk (SR1–4)

- **SR1** = No active suicidal thoughts.  
- **SR2** = Passive suicidal thoughts ("I wish I were dead").  
- **SR3** = Active thoughts with some intent (plan forming).  
- **SR4** = Clear intent, plan, and/or means; emergency.

In many countries:

- **Lifeline or general crisis line:** local number  
- **Suicide‑specific helpline:** local number  
- **Emergency services:** 000 / 911 / 112 (as appropriate)

---

### 3.3. Disaster Readiness (DR1–3)

- **DR1** = Normal / Advice level.  
- **DR2** = Prepare / Watch and Act (pack bag, charge devices).  
- **DR3** = Emergency Warning / Act now (evacuate or shelter).

---

## 4. Notes
- The code is always `BxMxSx/SRx/DRx-YYYY-MM-DD/HHMM` with 1–4 on B, M, S and 1–4 on SR, 1–3 on DR.  
- The date and time field uses ISO 8601 format (YYYY-MM-DD) followed by 24-hour time (HHMM), separated by a forward slash.
- Keep this document on your device, print it, and share the key lines with your trusted people.
